# AI Ops Sprint Sales Kit

## One-Line Positioning

I help small B2B service teams automate lead handling and follow-up in 48 hours, so fewer inquiries get lost and less manual work is needed.

## Short Offer

48-Hour AI Operations Sprint

- Fixed scope.
- Fixed timeline.
- One workflow only.
- USD 500 first-client price.
- USD 250 upfront, USD 250 on delivery.

## Prospect Qualification

Qualified:

- The business sells B2B or high-ticket services.
- A single new customer is worth at least USD 500.
- Leads arrive through a form, email, booking page, LinkedIn, newsletter, or referrals.
- Follow-up is partly manual.
- The owner or operator can approve a small purchase quickly.

Disqualified:

- No clear offer.
- No visible lead channel.
- No budget.
- Regulated workflow requiring professional liability review.
- Wants speculative unpaid implementation.

## Diagnostic Questions

Use these before quoting:

1. Where do new inquiries currently come from?
2. Who sees them first?
3. How long does it usually take to reply?
4. What information is missing when a lead arrives?
5. What makes a lead high-priority?
6. Where do follow-ups get tracked?
7. How many leads went cold in the last 30 days?
8. What is one new customer worth?
9. What tools do you already use?
10. If this saved 5 hours per week or recovered one deal, what would that be worth?

## Reply Handling

If they say "what do you automate?":

> Usually one of three things: lead triage, follow-up drafting, or consultation prep. I would look at your current flow first, then pick the smallest workflow that saves time or reduces missed opportunities.

If they ask for examples:

> Example: new inquiry arrives, AI summarizes the request, classifies priority, drafts a first reply, adds the lead to a follow-up queue, and reminds the owner if no response happens after a set period.

If they ask for price:

> First-client sprint price is USD 500 fixed: USD 250 to start and USD 250 after delivery. Timeline is 48 hours after I have the needed access and examples.

If they ask for a free build:

> I can do a free diagnosis, but implementation is paid. The sprint is intentionally small and fixed-price so it does not turn into an open-ended consulting project.

If they say "send details":

> I can send a one-page scope. The only thing I need first is where your leads come from, who follows up, and what gets missed most often.

## Handoff Checklist

Before delivery:

- Confirm exact workflow.
- Confirm input source.
- Confirm output destination.
- Confirm success example.
- Confirm what must not be automated.

On delivery:

- Show before/after.
- Test with three real examples.
- Provide one-page operating guide.
- Record known limitations.
- Ask for retainer or referral.
