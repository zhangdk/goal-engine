# 2026-04-12 AI Ops Revenue Sprint

## Goal

Earn the first paid deposit for an AI automation service within 7 days.

Success means a real buyer pays money. Replies, calls, positive feedback, and demos do not count unless they convert into a paid deposit.

## Execution Owner

This document currently records a `Codex baseline` execution, not an OpenClaw Agent evolution run.

The market research, prospect selection, public email extraction, outreach copy, and first Gmail Web send were performed by the supervising Codex session. They are useful as a baseline and as experimental setup material, but they must not be counted as evidence that an OpenClaw Agent can discover customers or evolve its strategy.

For an OpenClaw Agent evolution run, use this only as context for constraints and scoring. The OpenClaw Agent must find fresh targets, make its own bounded attempt, write failures back to Goal Engine, and demonstrate changed behavior through the recovery packet.

## Hard Constraints

- Legal, honest, permission-based execution only.
- No spam, fake scarcity, account abuse, scraping behind logins, deception, or platform rule evasion.
- No regulated advice claims in medical, legal, tax, investment, or insurance contexts.
- No external sending, posting, signing up, or payment action without explicit human approval.

## Primary Path

Sell a productized 48-hour AI operations sprint to small B2B service teams.

The offer:

> I help small B2B service teams automate one lead handling or follow-up bottleneck in 48 hours, so fewer inquiries get lost and less manual work is needed.

## Success Criteria

- Day 1: offer, target list, scripts, diagnostic checklist, and invoice language ready.
- Day 2-3: 30-50 approved prospects contacted.
- Day 3-5: 3 qualified replies or diagnostic conversations.
- Day 5-7: 1 paid deposit of USD 250-750.

## Why This Path

Fast revenue comes from selling a painful outcome to reachable buyers, not from building a product first.

This path has the best speed-to-cash profile because:

- The buyer already has a business and budget.
- The pain maps to money: missed leads, slow replies, manual follow-up.
- Delivery can be scoped to one workflow and completed in 48 hours.
- AI automation demand is visible in current freelance and small-business adoption signals.
- The first sale can happen through direct outreach without waiting for platform ranking or audience growth.

## Market Signals

Facts:

- Upwork's 2025 skills report lists generative AI and scripting/automation among high-demand freelance skill areas.
- Goldman Sachs reported in 2026 that small businesses are broadly adopting AI but still need training and support.
- Fiverr's 2025 business trends index reported strong buyer interest in AI agents and AI-enabled freelance services.

Inference:

Small businesses are interested in AI, but many do not have internal implementation capacity. A tightly scoped implementation sprint is easier to buy than an abstract AI consulting engagement.

Recommendation:

Do not sell "AI strategy." Sell one operational fix with a fixed price, fixed timeline, and visible before/after.

## Buyer Profile

Best-fit buyer:

- Small B2B service company, agency, consulting team, recruiter, training provider, or software service firm.
- Has public proof of client acquisition: contact forms, booked calls, case studies, webinars, newsletters, or sales pages.
- Sells services worth at least USD 500 per customer.
- Has a founder, operator, sales lead, or agency owner reachable by email or LinkedIn.
- Likely has manual lead triage, delayed follow-up, or duplicated content/admin work.

Avoid:

- Low-ticket retail.
- Heavily regulated workflows.
- Buyers asking for unpaid implementation before payment.
- Prospects who only want a general AI chat.

## Productized Offer

Name: 48-Hour AI Operations Sprint

Opening price:

- First client: USD 500 fixed.
- Payment: USD 250 upfront, USD 250 on delivery.
- Acceptable first win: any paid deposit from USD 250 upward.

Standard price after proof:

- USD 1,500 fixed.
- Optional retainer: USD 750/month for monitoring, prompt improvement, and workflow expansion.

Scope:

- Map the current lead intake or follow-up process.
- Pick one bottleneck.
- Build one working automation.
- Test it with real examples.
- Deliver a one-page operating guide.
- Provide a 30-minute handoff.

Out of scope:

- Full CRM migration.
- Multi-channel outbound engine.
- Ads management.
- Custom SaaS.
- Ongoing support unless converted to retainer.

## Default Sprint Deliverables

Choose one:

1. Lead intake triage
   - New inquiries are captured into a structured sheet or CRM view.
   - Each lead receives a priority tag, next action, and draft reply.

2. Follow-up assistant
   - Unanswered leads are detected.
   - A personalized follow-up draft is generated.
   - The owner receives a daily follow-up queue.

3. Consultation prep packet
   - New booking details are summarized.
   - Relevant context, objections, and questions are prepared before the call.

4. Content repurposing workflow
   - Long notes, calls, or articles become short posts, newsletters, or client updates.

Recommended first deliverable:

Lead intake triage plus follow-up assistant. It is closest to revenue and easiest to explain.

## Execution Cadence

Daily scoreboard:

- Prospects identified.
- Personalized first messages drafted.
- Messages approved and sent.
- Replies received.
- Diagnostics completed.
- Deposits requested.
- Deposits paid.

Kill rule:

If 50 well-targeted, personalized touches produce zero replies, do not change the offer randomly. First tighten the niche and improve personalization evidence. Only switch path after documenting why the buyer segment did not respond.

## Approval Gate

The agent can do without additional approval:

- Research public prospect data.
- Draft outreach.
- Prepare diagnostic notes.
- Create local documents and templates.
- Build sample automations in local files.

The agent must stop for approval before:

- Sending any email, DM, form submission, or public post.
- Creating or using payment links.
- Registering accounts.
- Spending money.
- Using personal contacts or private data.
